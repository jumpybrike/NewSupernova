The Foundation of Modern Science Fiction Outfit 700

Isaac Asimov's Foundation series stands as one of the defining works of the Golden Age. Drawing inspiration from Gibbon's Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire, Asimov crafted a sweeping narrative of galactic civilization that would influence generations of writers and thinkers. Source Serif 4 400

The concept of psychohistory—a mathematical sociology capable of predicting the behaviour of large populations—remains one of science fiction's most provocative thought experiments. It asks us to consider whether humanity's future might be not just predictable, but guidable.

"Violence is the last refuge of the incompetent." Outfit 500 — Salvor Hardin, Foundation (1951) Space Grotesk 400

The trilogy's influence extends far beyond literature. Elon Musk has cited it as formative to his thinking about civilizational resilience; Paul Krugman credits it with inspiring his career in economics. Few works of fiction can claim such tangible impact on the real world.
